Medication Guidelines and Safety

General Medication Safety:
- Always take medications as prescribed by your doctor
- Never share prescription medications with others
- Store medications in a cool, dry place away from children
- Check expiration dates regularly
- Keep a list of all medications you take
- Inform doctors about all medications including supplements

Common Pain Medications:
Acetaminophen (Tylenol):
- Used for pain and fever
- Maximum dose: 4000mg per day for adults
- Take with food if upset stomach occurs
- Avoid alcohol while taking

Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin):
- Used for pain, inflammation, and fever
- Take with food to prevent stomach upset
- Maximum dose: 3200mg per day for adults
- Not recommended for people with kidney problems or bleeding disorders

Antibiotic Guidelines:
- Complete the full course even if feeling better
- Take at evenly spaced intervals
- Some require taking with food, others on empty stomach
- Do not save antibiotics for later use
- Common antibiotics: Amoxicillin, Azithromycin, Ciprofloxacin

Blood Pressure Medications:
- Take at the same time each day
- Do not stop suddenly without doctor approval
- Monitor blood pressure regularly at home
- Report side effects to doctor
- Common types: ACE inhibitors, Beta-blockers, Diuretics
