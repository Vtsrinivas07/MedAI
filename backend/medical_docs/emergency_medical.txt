Emergency Medical Situations

When to Call 911:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Sudden severe headache
- Sudden weakness or numbness (especially one side)
- Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizures
- Poisoning or overdose

Heart Attack Warning Signs:
- Chest pain or discomfort (pressure, squeezing, fullness)
- Pain spreading to shoulders, neck, arms, or jaw
- Shortness of breath
- Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
- Women may have atypical symptoms

Stroke Warning Signs (FAST):
F - Face drooping (ask person to smile)
A - Arm weakness (ask person to raise both arms)
S - Speech difficulty (ask person to repeat simple phrase)
T - Time to call 911 immediately

Basic First Aid:
Cuts and Scrapes:
- Wash hands first
- Stop bleeding with clean cloth and pressure
- Clean wound with water
- Apply antibiotic ointment
- Cover with bandage
- Watch for signs of infection

Burns:
- Cool burn with cool (not ice cold) water
- Cover with sterile gauze
- Do not apply ice, butter, or ointments
- Seek medical help for severe burns

Choking:
- Encourage coughing if person can cough
- Perform Heimlich maneuver if cannot breathe
- Call 911 if object not removed
